2 · Method & Definition

Core definition (nominal, undiscounted)

Cumulative cashflow through year N: CumCF_N = Σ CF_i (i = 1 … N) Cashflow payback = smallest N where CumCF_N ≥ initial investment

No (1+r) factor, no reinvestment — every euro of cashflow counts as exactly one euro. Payback therefore depends only on the fundamentals of the property (net rent ÷ investment) and makes no assumption about a capital market that no one controls.

What was removed versus v3 (MSCI EM payback)

  • ❌ MSCI EM reinvestment logic — cashflow flows into no ETF; it is simply accumulated.
  • ❌ CAGR scenarios (5 % / 7 % / 9 %) — no compounding, so no range needed.
  • ❌ Geopolitical thesis (US housing, AI bubble, Hormuz/petrodollar) — belongs to the question "what to do with the cashflow", not to the payback question.
  • ❌ Wealth projections via the ETF pot — replaced by the cumulative cashflow sum.

Retained: market screening, regulation/taxes, climate risks, scoring matrix, deep dives, exit strategy — version-independent and still valid.

Second metric: break-even with sale

NetSale_N = purchase price × 1.02^N × 0.95 − capital-gains tax (35 % on gain, only if N < 10) Break-even = smallest N where CumCF_N + NetSale_N ≥ initial investment

This metric lands early for every city (year 2–4). No trick: the property retains its value, and you could sell it at almost any time for ~the purchase price. The real "capital at risk" that only cashflow can recover is closing costs + furnishing (~€35–40k) — the purchase price sits inside the sellable asset.

10 · Honest Limits & Sources

  • Occupancy assumed flat — a climate-shock year with occ -10pp would delay payback by 2–3 years.
  • Nominal payback is the default — those who value opportunity cost heavily should use the discounted column (§7).
  • No holding-structure optimisation — could save 5–8 % tax and improve payback (separate legal analysis).
  • Own use not modelled — 1–2 weeks/year of personal use lowers occ by ~3–5 % and lengthens payback.
  • What happens to the cashflow after payback is deliberately out of scope — that was the question of the previous version (ETF reinvestment) and was intentionally removed here.
